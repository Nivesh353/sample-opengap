# Write Article

Write a comprehensive blog article based on the topic and the research notes gathered in the research-topic step.

## Step 1: Review research notes
Read through the complete research notes from the previous step. Identify:
- The central thesis or main takeaway for the article
- The logical structure of the body (what sections make sense given the research)
- The most compelling facts, examples, or insights to highlight
- An engaging angle for the introduction hook

## Step 2: Plan the article structure
Outline the article before writing:
- **Introduction** — hook the reader, state what the article covers, establish why it matters
- **Body sections** — 3-5 clearly organized sections, each with a descriptive heading
- **Conclusion** — summarize key takeaways, leave the reader with a lasting thought or call to action

## Step 3: Write the article
Write a complete, engaging blog article following the outline. Apply these writing principles:
- Open with a compelling hook that draws the reader in immediately
- Use clear, active language — avoid jargon unless writing for a technical audience (in which case, define terms)
- Make each section self-contained but connected to the overall narrative
- Use concrete examples and specifics rather than vague generalities
- Write a strong conclusion that ties the article together and gives the reader something to remember or do

The article should be thorough and well-developed — not a thin outline dressed up as prose. Aim for genuine depth and reader value.

## Step 4: Announce completion
State:
> "✍️ Draft complete. Proceeding to review."

Retain the full draft — the review-and-finalize skill needs it for editing.
